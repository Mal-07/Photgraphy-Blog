My First Shoot:

One fall I decided I was going to have a photoshoot. Not professional, just wanted to get some pretty pictures and I had a theme. I spent a week or two gathering ideas, planning, communicating with my friends to be models and brainstorm, and researching.The shoot was great! It was a beautiful day, everything went according to plan, I got some really good pictures, and once I get permission to share them, I'll add them to the Gallery, so head on over there to see other beautiful pictures I've taken!

My Gear:

I don't have a thousand dollar camera, a tripod or anything fancy. I have a cell phone with a good camera, however, and a basic knowledge in photo editing. I started out in photography with 4-H, and spent a year doing photography and learning how to get decent pictures on a phone. Then, after that year, I changed projects and started doing photography for the practice. I worked on my weak points, learned to find new perspectives, and think outside the box.
Moral of the story: You don't always need to have fancy equipment. Sometimes the basics and some Youtube will work.
